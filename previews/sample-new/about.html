About ALN

A media company for people who take the question seriously.

Mission

Signal over spectacle.

Aliens Last Night covers Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena through three lenses: intelligence, defense, and public policy. We talk to the pilots, scientists, lawmakers, and analysts who have been closest to the evidence.

No hype. No recycled lore. What is verifiable, what is contested, and what matters next.

Editorial standard

Four rules that govern everything we publish.

01
Sourced or it does not exist.
Every claim ties to a named source, a primary document, or sensor data we can show on screen.
02
Firsthand experience or expertise only.
We book the people who flew the intercept, ran the program, drafted the bill, or examined the data.
03
Show our work.
FOIA documents, flight tracks, hearing transcripts, and frame-by-frame footage are surfaced in line. Viewers evaluate the evidence themselves.
04
Restraint over hype.
Where evidence is incomplete, we say so. Where mainstream narratives are wrong, we explain why.

What we cover

The phenomenon does not live in any one beat.

Intelligence
Intel
Declassified programs, whistleblower testimony, and document trails. We follow the paper from FOIA request to congressional record.
Defense
DoD
Pilot encounters, sensor data, and military incident reports. Radar and FLIR evidence read by people who can read it.
Policy
Hill
Congressional hearings, legislation, oversight, and transparency efforts. Bills, markups, public depositions.

Who we feature

People with firsthand experience. That is the bar.

Pilots

Navy aviators, Air Force interceptors, and commercial captains with logged encounters.

Scientists

Astrophysicists, radar engineers, materials scientists, and atmospheric researchers.

Lawmakers

Members of Congress and senior staff working on UAP oversight, transparency, and budget.

Analysts

Former IC officers, defense contractors, and FOIA archivists who can read the documents.
